Restaurant Ryn

Arrive by air. Dine at Chef Matthew Cooper’s farm-to-table table.

The finest table in Northwest Arkansas is just three minutes from the runway — if you know how to arrive.

Lift off from Thaden Field in Bentonville and, before you’ve finished taking in the view, you’re touching down. Restaurant Ryn — Chef Matthew Cooper’s acclaimed farm-to-table destination — sits barely three miles from the field. The drive might take twenty minutes; the helicopter takes three.

Inside, Chef Cooper’s kitchen turns the region’s finest seasonal produce, local proteins, and foraged ingredients into a tasting menu that changes with the land. Every plate is a quiet argument for why Northwest Arkansas belongs on any serious food map. We arrange your reservation in advance and let the restaurant know how you’re getting there — which tends to make for an especially warm welcome.

When dinner is done, your pilot is waiting. A short flight back under the Ozark sky and the evening closes on a note almost no one else can claim: you flew to dinner and home again, and the whole thing felt effortless.

What’s included

  • Round-trip helicopter transfer from Thaden Field, Bentonville
  • Reservation arranged in advance on your behalf
  • Chef’s tasting menu or à la carte — your choice
  • Private table with advance notice of your arrival
  • Optional sunset scenic loop en route
  • Return flight home before the night’s over

How the day flies

Four steps, one unforgettable evening.

Depart Thaden Field

Meet your pilot in Bentonville and lift straight off — no traffic, no parking, no waiting.

A three-minute hop

Cross the rooftops of Bentonville at altitude and set down moments later, reservation already secured.

Sit down to the tasting menu

Chef Cooper’s kitchen takes it from here — seasonal courses, local sourcing, and a table that knows you came by air.

Fly home under the stars

Your pilot meets you after the last course and flies you back to Thaden Field while the Ozark night opens up below.
