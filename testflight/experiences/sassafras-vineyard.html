◆ Estate Winery

Sassafras Springs Vineyard

Private tastings among the vines, a short hop south of the runway.

Northwest Arkansas grows more than mountain trails — it grows estate wine, and a helicopter is the most civilised way to arrive.

Lift off from Thaden Field in Bentonville and follow the hills south. In roughly fifteen minutes you cross into wine country — rolling vineyard rows, cedar-post trellises heavy with Ozark-grown fruit — and set down at Sassafras Springs Vineyard & Winery near Springdale. The estate sits on a quiet country road at the edge of the property, a long way from any highway.

A private tasting is waiting for you in the tasting room. Work through flights of estate-grown red, white, and rosé poured by the people who made them, with a cheese and charcuterie board that earns its place on the table. There is no rush, no last call — the helicopter does not leave until you are ready.

No designated driver required, no rental car, no negotiating a two-lane highway after a second pour. We tuck your bottles into the cargo hold and fly you home with the afternoon still ahead of you.

What’s included

  • Round-trip helicopter transfer from Thaden Field, Bentonville
  • Private estate tasting at Sassafras Springs Vineyard
  • Flight over NWA wine country — aerial views you won’t find on the map
  • Cheese & charcuterie pairing in the tasting room
  • Bottles to take home, stowed safely in the helicopter
  • Relaxed half-day pacing — no rushing between venues
