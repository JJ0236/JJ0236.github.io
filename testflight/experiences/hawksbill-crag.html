◆ A Meditative Walk

Hawksbill Crag

A quiet walk to Arkansas’s most photographed bluff.

Some places ask nothing of you except to show up and be still — Hawksbill Crag is one of them.

Lift off from Thaden Field in Bentonville and trade the highway for a flight north over the rolling hardwood canopy of the Ozark National Forest. In roughly half an hour the Upper Buffalo wilderness spreads below you — one of the least-trodden corners of Arkansas, its limestone bluffs wrapped in oak and hickory that burn amber and crimson come autumn.

We set down near the trailhead and the pace shifts entirely. A gentle three-mile round trip winds through cathedral forest before the trees pull back and the land simply ends at Hawksbill Crag — a narrow finger of sandstone jutting out over the valley, nothing below it but air and the slow-moving Buffalo River watershed far below. It is the most photographed spot in Arkansas, and standing on it you understand exactly why.

We time the flight for sunrise or the last golden hour, when the light comes in low and the shadows in the valley are long and everything is quietly extraordinary. There is nothing to do but look, breathe, and let the morning or the evening do its work.

What’s included

  • Round-trip helicopter transfer from Thaden Field, Bentonville
  • Guided ~3-mile round-trip walk to the crag and back
  • Sunrise or golden-hour timing arranged around the forecast
  • Unhurried time to sit quietly on the bluff
  • Optional packed picnic to enjoy at the overlook
  • Small groups only — never more than six guests

How the day flies

Four steps, one unforgettable morning.

Depart Thaden Field

Meet your pilot in Bentonville and lift off just before dawn — no lines, no crowds, just the quiet hum of rotors over the Ozarks.

Land near the trailhead

Touch down in the Upper Buffalo wilderness and step straight onto the forest path — the helicopter ride is already half the view.

Walk to the crag

A peaceful three-mile round trip through old-growth hardwood forest, the trail rising gently until the trees give way to open sky.

Stillness on the bluff

Sit at the edge of Hawksbill Crag as the light pours over the valley — no agenda, no schedule, just the view and the silence.
