Crystal Mining

Dig for Ozark quartz in a working private mine — and keep every crystal you pull from the rock.

The Ouachitas hold some of the finest quartz on earth — and a helicopter is the only graceful way in.

Lift off from Thaden Field in Bentonville and trade the highway for a forty-minute flight over ridge after forested ridge. Below you, the Ouachita Mountains run east to west — the only range in North America that does — their quartz veins formed under heat and pressure some 300 million years ago.

You set down at a private working mine where a seasoned local miner is waiting. He walks you through the geology, hands you a pick, and points you at the freshly worked tailings. Then it’s just you, the rock, and the particular thrill of brushing red clay off a clear point that no one has ever seen.

Every crystal you find is yours to keep — raw, unpolished, and pulled from the ground by your own hands. We bag your haul, fly you home, and the whole Ozark afternoon fits inside a single day.

What’s included

  • Round-trip helicopter transfer from Thaden Field, Bentonville
  • Private guided dig session with a working miner
  • All tools provided — just bring sturdy shoes
  • Keep everything you find, however much it is
  • Lunch at a nearby local smokehouse
  • Half-day and full-day options available

How the day flies

Four steps, one unforgettable afternoon.

Depart Thaden Field

Meet your pilot in Bentonville and lift off straight over the Ozarks — no security lines, no shuttle.

Land at the mine

Touch down at a private claim deep in the Ouachitas where your guide is already waiting.

Dig with a guide

Learn to read the veins, work the fresh tailings, and unearth clear quartz points by hand.

Keep your haul

We bag everything you find, break for a smokehouse lunch, and fly you home by evening.
