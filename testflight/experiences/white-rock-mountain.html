White Rock Mountain

100+ miles of private trail — and nobody else on it.

A hundred miles of trail tucked into the Ozark National Forest — reached in under an hour by air.

Lift off from Thaden Field in Bentonville and follow the ridgelines south. In roughly forty-five minutes the helicopter sets you down at a remote trailhead on White Rock Mountain — roughly 47 miles from home as the crow flies, worlds apart in feel. Down below, the highway stays empty; you haven’t seen a car since takeoff.

White Rock Mountain is a private trail playground woven through the national forest, with more than 100 miles of purpose-built singletrack and ridge-top gravel. Routes are curated by ability: flow trails with long sweeping berms for cruisers, technical rock gardens for riders who want to earn it, and wide gravel ridge roads that trade difficulty for views that stretch county to county. Motocross options open the same terrain to a different kind of power.

When the riding is done, the bluff-top cabins are waiting — perched on the escarpment with the forest falling away below. Stay overnight or loop back; either way the helicopter handles retrieval and the only thing you carry home is the good kind of tired.

What’s included

  • Helicopter drop at a remote trailhead from Thaden Field, Bentonville
  • Curated route matched to your ability and riding style
  • Mountain bikes and e-bikes available — or bring your own
  • Motocross options for those who prefer two wheels and throttle
  • Bluff-top cabin access for overnight stays
  • Shuttle or loop retrieval by helicopter when you’re finished

How the day flies

Four steps, one unforgettable afternoon.

Depart Thaden Field

Meet your pilot in Bentonville and lift off straight over the Ozarks — no security lines, no shuttle.

Drop at a private trailhead

Touch down at a remote entry point deep in the national forest, gear ready, trail deserted.

Ride your route

Follow your curated line — flow trail, technical rock, ridge gravel, or open throttle — at your own pace.

Cabin on the bluff (optional)

End the day above the treeline in a bluff-top cabin, or flag the helicopter for retrieval and head home by evening.
